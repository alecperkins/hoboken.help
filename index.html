How to Help Hoboken

Information and resources to help the Hoboken community and our neighbors during the COVID-19 crisis.

Last updated: Tuesday April 7, 2020 @ 4:00pm


Stay informed

Sign up to the Nixle system to receive alerts by email and/or SMS text direct from the City of Hoboken.

Stay inside

“Flatten the curve.” Staying inside and reducing physical contact with others slows the spread of the virus. This helps the hospital system and its related supply lines keep up. Every bit of reduced contact helps. Even a single broken link in the chain of viral transmission has an impact.

Get counted

Fill out your 2020 Census form. This online form takes just 10 minutes, but will shape the next decade of your representation in government, federal funding, and data that drives decisions.

Get tested if sick

Riverside Medical is operating a testing facility open to symptomatic residents in Hoboken. [Nixle alert]

Residents with symptoms of COVID-19 must call 201-420-5621 [tel] in advance to schedule an appointment. No walk-in testing will be permitted without an appointment.

Make masks

Make and wear masks when you do have to go outside. Even simple cloth masks help slow the spread of the virus in the community when worn properly and combined with frequent hand washing.

Handle masks by their ties or loops, wash them with detergent and promptly dry after use. Wash your hands after handling the mask.
